Charles Robert Darwin

The man that developed the theory of evolution by natural selection.

Here's a time-line of Charles Darwin's life:

  • 1809 - Charles Darwin was born on February 12 in Shrewsbury, England.
  • 1818 - Attended Shrewsbury School, a boarding school known for its classical curriculum.
  • 1825-1827 - Enrolled at the University of Edinburgh to study medicine, but found it uninteresting and disturbing (due to surgery without anesthesia).
  • 1828 - Entered Christ’s College, Cambridge, intending to become a clergyman. During this time, he became deeply interested in natural science.
  • 1831-1836 - Darwin embarked on the HMS Beagle as a naturalist. The voyage, particularly his observations in the Galápagos Islands, profoundly influenced his thinking about species and their development.
  • 1839 - Published his journal from the Beagle expedition, "The Voyage of the Beagle", which brought him initial fame as a naturalist.
  • 1839 - Married his cousin, Emma Wedgwood. Together, they had 10 children, many of whom made significant contributions to various scientific fields.
  • 1842 - Moved to Down House in Kent, where Darwin would spend the rest of his life conducting research and writing.
  • 1859 - Published "On the Origin of Species", introducing the theory of natural selection. The book sparked widespread debate and transformed biology.
  • 1871 - Published "The Descent of Man", where he applied evolutionary theory to human evolution and emphasized the shared ancestry between humans and other animals.
  • 1882 - Darwin died on April 19 at the age of 73. He was buried in Westminster Abbey, an unusual honor for someone who challenged religious thought in such profound ways.
